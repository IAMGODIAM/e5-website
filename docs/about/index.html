About E5 Enclave — Black-Led Nonprofit Think Tank | Liberty City Miami

A lineage-led think tank comprised of a Coalition of the Willing.

E5 Enclave Incorporated is a 501(c)(3) public charity incorporated in the state of Florida on June 2, 2024, in Liberty City, Miami. We are a lineage-led think tank — convening builders, educators, farmers, technologists, advocates, and community leaders to repair, rise, and govern the lineage's future.

Who We Are

E5 Enclave is the architecture the work has always needed and never had.

For three centuries the obligations of emancipation went unrecorded — not because they were unknown, but because the infrastructure of remedy was never built. E5 Enclave is that infrastructure. The Coalition is the network. The Five Pillars are the doctrine. The programs are the proof.

We are operated by an agentic-first staff — AI agents under human direction — producing institutional-scale output from a deliberately lean operating team built to outlast cycles. We are the world's first agentic-first staffed nonprofit, declared June 11, 2024.

We do not promise a finish line. We promise discipline of scope — naming precisely what we are doing, and what we are not. We promise coordination over competition. We promise that what is measured cannot be dismissed.

The center holds. The work is underway.

Our Founding Doctrine — Five Tenets

I. Empirical Integrity. Decisions grounded in dual-source-verified evidence. What is measured cannot be dismissed.

II. Sovereign Capacity. Communities own their data, their institutions, and their futures. We coordinate — we do not absorb.

III. Black-First. Built by and for the lineage. Coalition allies strengthen the work. The center holds.

IV. Radical Transparency. Substantial research published openly. Operating model in plain language. Records made public.

V. Intergenerational Accountability. Built for the people who will inherit it. We are stewards, not owners.

The Five Pillars

The structure of the harm is the structure of the remedy.

  • Environmental — Food sovereignty, land stewardship, agriculture, and anti-food-apartheid infrastructure. Flagship: Lineage Farms / FarmBlock.
  • Economic — Business formation, government contracting, cooperative ownership, and circular economics.
  • Educational — Private education, STEM, emerging technologies, leadership, and lineage-centered curriculum. Flagship: McCartney Academy.
  • Engagement — Civic education, public policy literacy, media, storytelling, and mobilization. Flagship: Block to the Boardroom.
  • Emancipated — The unfinished obligations of freedom. Documented obligations become policy. Campaign: Restitution 246.

Our Founder

Israel Lee Armstead — Chairman & Founder, E5 Enclave Incorporated.

Born and raised in Liberty City, Miami. Graduate of Charles R. Drew K-8 (2001) and Miami Northwestern Senior High (2005). Former General Councilor and Candidacy Member, Urantia Book Fellowship. Executive Director, E5 Enclave Incorporated. Chairman, IAMGODIAM.

The McCartney Academy at Charles R. Drew is named in honor of Ralph McCartney — Israel's grand-uncle, a Liberty City elder who served on the Miami-Dade County Public Schools board committee and worked as a security guard at Charles R. Drew K-8 during Israel's years there. The Enclave carries that lineage forward.
