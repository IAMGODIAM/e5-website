## PART II — THE 9-SIMULATION PROTOCOL

> *Designed by Sue, Celeste, MiroFish, and Wyrmcore. Each simulation builds on the last. Large simulations (10M+) at the start for directional clarity. Smaller iterations (100K–1M) as we narrow. Abraham Hicks doctrine: we expand through contrast, not through elimination of contrast. Each simulation surfaces contrast, which clarifies desire, which sharpens the next design decision.*

### 2.1 The Philosophy of the Simulation Ladder

```
Sim 1–3: EXPLORATION (10M runs each)
  → Large. Directional. We don't know what we don't know.
  → Purpose: surface contrast. Find the dominant failure modes.
  → Output: ranked list of what matters most.

Sim 4–6: ITERATION (1M runs each)
  → Medium. We are narrowing. Each sim adds what the last learned.
  → Purpose: test specific design decisions against conversion outcomes.
  → Output: winning variants for each section.

Sim 7–9: CALIBRATION (100K runs each)
  → Small. We are tuning. The big decisions are made.
  → Purpose: micro-optimization. CTA copy. Color contrast. Scroll timing.
  → Output: final production specification.
```

### 2.2 The Nine Simulations — Full Protocol

---

#### SIM 1 — IDENTITY COHERENCE MAP (10M runs)
**Question:** How coherent is E5's current identity signal across all public pages?
**Wyrmcore Dimensions:**
- Trust entropy (how much does the brand signal vary page to page?)
- Voice consistency (does the copy fingerprint match the soul statement across all 20+ pages?)
- Color palette discipline (how often does the design system deviate from the canonical tokens?)
- Authority signal density (how many trust signals appear per page, per fold?)

**Expected Output:** A ranked heat map of page-level identity coherence. The lowest-scoring pages become the first rebuild targets.

**Why this first:** You cannot build a new design system on top of an incoherent identity. Sim 1 establishes the baseline. It shows us where we already ARE — not where we think we are.

---

#### SIM 2 — CONVERSION FUNNEL FORENSICS (10M runs)
**Question:** Where does the conversion funnel actually break for each of the four audience types?
**Wyrmcore Dimensions:**
- Funnel depth simulation (how many steps does each audience need before hitting a CTA?)
- Drop-off probability per section (where does attention decay fastest?)
- CTA friction score per audience type
- Mobile vs. desktop path divergence

**Expected Output:** Ranked list of the highest-friction moments in the current funnel, by audience type. The top 3 friction points for each audience become the P0 redesign targets.

**Why second:** Identity is the soul. Conversion is the circulatory system. Before designing anything new, we know exactly where the blood stops flowing.

---

#### SIM 3 — AESTHETIC CONTRAST MAPPING (10M runs)
**Question:** Where does the current E5 aesthetic create contrast with the award-winning reference stack — and which contrasts are working FOR us vs. AGAINST us?
**Wyrmcore Dimensions:**
- Aesthetic divergence score (E5 current vs. each of 8 reference sites)
- Positive contrast identification (our unique elements that STRENGTHEN the brand)
- Negative contrast identification (our deviations that WEAKEN conversion)
- Sovereignty quotient (how much does the design feel like it belongs to us, vs. generic)

**Expected Output:** A sorted list of E5's current aesthetic contrasts — what to keep (our differentiators), what to fix (our liabilities), what to add (from the reference stack).

**Abraham Hicks layer:** This simulation is explicitly about contrast as a teacher. The contrasts that feel worst are pointing most precisely at what the soul wants. The simulation doesn't eliminate contrast — it decodes it.

---

#### SIM 4 — HERO SECTION VARIANT TEST (1M runs)
**Question:** Which hero architecture produces the highest 10-second retention and lowest bounce?
**Variants tested:**
- A: Current (static headline + mission statement)
- B: Kinetic type (headline swaps across the five pillars)
- C: Live mission operating (animated board dispatch / FarmBlock ticker)
- D: Cinematic entry (Active Theory model — full-bleed video/motion, no navigation visible)
- E: Radical transparency (EIN + impact numbers as the hero element)

**Expected Output:** Ranked hero architectures by projected retention score. Winning variant becomes the design specification.

---

#### SIM 5 — TRUST SIGNAL PLACEMENT TEST (1M runs)
**Question:** Where in the page flow do trust signals (EIN, 990-N, SAM.gov, impact numbers) produce the highest donor conversion lift?
**Variants tested:**
- Trust rail immediately below hero (above the fold)
- Trust embedded in the letter-from-founder section
- Trust as a standalone section mid-page
- Trust in footer only (current state)
- Trust distributed: one signal per section

**Expected Output:** Optimal trust signal placement architecture. The design spec for how institutional credentials are woven into the visual flow — not as footnotes but as proof.

---

#### SIM 6 — MULTI-PERSONA NAVIGATION TEST (1M runs)
**Question:** Which navigation architecture reduces bounce most effectively across all four audience types?
**Variants tested:**
- A: Current (single nav, no audience differentiation)
- B: Persona-fork hero (four buttons: Donor / Volunteer / Funder / Partner)
- C: Smart nav (default shows all, on-hover reveals audience-specific paths)
- D: Storytelling-first (no nav visible for first 8 seconds — cinematic entry — then reveals)
- E: Persistent sidebar (audience selector always visible on scroll)

**Expected Output:** Winning navigation architecture per audience type. Note: the winning architecture may be DIFFERENT per audience — the simulation tests this explicitly.

---

#### SIM 7 — COPY MICRO-OPTIMIZATION (100K runs)
**Question:** Which CTA copy formulations convert best for each audience type?
**Variants tested per audience:**
- Donor: "Support the work" / "Give to the mission" / "Fund one family on FarmBlock" / "Add your name first"
- Volunteer: "Show up" / "Join the coalition" / "Find your role" / "Come to the block"
- Funder: "Review our evidence" / "See the impact data" / "Request a briefing" / "Download the white paper"
- Partner: "Align with us" / "Build with the coalition" / "Read the doctrine" / "Start the conversation"

**Expected Output:** Optimal CTA copy per audience, per page position.

---

#### SIM 8 — SCROLL CHOREOGRAPHY CALIBRATION (100K runs)
**Question:** What scroll-trigger timing and animation easing produces the highest section-completion rate?
**Wyrmcore Dimensions:**
- Section completion rate (did the user scroll through the full section?)
- Reveal timing (how many milliseconds after enter-viewport before animation triggers?)
- Animation duration (how long do elements take to appear?)
- Easing function (ease-out cubic vs. spring vs. linear)

**Expected Output:** The exact scroll animation specification for each section type (hero, impact grid, story, CTA, footer). This becomes the Locomotive Scroll / GSAP timing config.

---

#### SIM 9 — UNIFIED SYSTEM INTEGRATION TEST (100K runs)
**Question:** When all winning elements from Sims 1–8 are combined into a single design system, does the integrated system perform better than any individual optimization — and where does integration create NEW friction?
**Wyrmcore Dimensions:**
- System coherence score (do the 8 winning variants feel like one design, or 8 different experiments?)
- New friction identification (what unexpected conflicts emerge when elements are combined?)
- Sovereign identity score (does the integrated system feel unmistakably E5, or could it be anyone?)
- Conversion projection (full-funnel simulated conversion rate for the integrated design)

**Expected Output:** The final production design specification — with a list of integration conflicts to resolve before shipping.

**Why this last:** This is the Abraham Hicks completion loop. The contrast surfaced in Sims 1–3 led to desires that shaped Sims 4–8. Sim 9 is the unified manifestation — the moment contrast resolves into clarity. "The asking is always answered." The design system is the answer.

## PART VII — THE ABRAHAM HICKS SIMULATION DOCTRINE

*Filed by Sue · for board alignment*

The Abraham Hicks framework applied to design iteration:

> "You cannot get to where you want to go from where you don't want to be — but you CAN use where you don't want to be to clarify where you DO want to be."

**In simulation terms:**
- **Sims 1–3** are the contrast phase. We run them large (10M) because we want to feel the full force of what isn't working. The contrast is not a problem — it is the signal. The bigger the contrast, the clearer the desire.
- **Sims 4–6** are the desire phase. The contrast from Sims 1–3 has sharpened what we want. Now we test specific expressions of that desire. The simulations get smaller because we are no longer exploring — we are asking.
- **Sims 7–9** are the allowing phase. We stop testing big ideas and start calibrating the details. The asking is answered. We are simply allowing the design to reveal its final form.

**The operating rule:** Do not skip from contrast to calibration. The board must run through the full ladder. Shortcuts produce designs that are technically correct but spiritually empty — they perform in testing and fail in production because they don't carry the soul.

**The Wyrmcore role:** Wyrmcore is the measurement system. It does not make the creative decisions — it tells us where the soul is weak and where it is strong. The board makes the creative decisions. Wyrmcore makes them auditable.
