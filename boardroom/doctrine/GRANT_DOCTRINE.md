## PART 2 — THE PRE-FLIGHT CHECKLIST (MANDATORY — ALL SUBMISSIONS)

Percy and Scout must complete every item before a single word of the LOI is drafted.

### GATE 1 — PROGRAM STATUS
- [ ] Is the program currently OPEN and accepting proposals?
- [ ] What is the submission deadline?
- [ ] Is this a rolling deadline, annual cycle, or one-time?
- [ ] Source: funder's own website (not third-party aggregators — they lag by months)

### GATE 2 — ELIGIBILITY
- [ ] Does E5 Enclave meet every stated eligibility requirement?
- [ ] Religious affiliation requirements? (Koch = Catholic. NAAF = SBC. Check.)
- [ ] Geographic restrictions? (Some funders: Southeast only, Florida only, specific cities)
- [ ] Organizational size/budget minimums or maximums?
- [ ] Prior relationship required? (Invitation-only = relationship pipeline, not direct submit)
- [ ] 501(c)(3) status confirmed as acceptable? (Some want 509(a)(1) specifically)

### GATE 3 — SUBMISSION METHOD
- [ ] Email? Portal? Postal mail? Specific platform (Submittable, Fluxx, Foundant)?
- [ ] Attachment or inline? PDF or Word? Page limits? Font requirements?
- [ ] What specific documents does this funder require? (Not what we want to send — what THEY ask for)
- [ ] Is a phone call or relationship contact preferred before submitting?

### GATE 4 — FIT SCORING (MiroFish gates this)
- [ ] What is the funder's stated priority area? Quote their exact language.
- [ ] How does E5/BDI/Gospel of Love map to that exact language?
- [ ] What did they fund in the last 2 years? (Check 990s on Candid/ProPublica)
- [ ] MiroFish confidence score: minimum 75% to proceed to drafting

### GATE 5 — CONTACT VERIFICATION
- [ ] Is the submission email address current and correct?
- [ ] Has the program officer or contact person been identified?
- [ ] Is there a pre-submission inquiry process available?

**All 5 gates must be GREEN before Percy writes a single sentence.**

## PART 3 — DOCUMENT ARCHITECTURE (Atlas Standard)

### What a Complete Grant Submission Package Looks Like

The submission package varies by funder type. Here are the three standard configurations:

#### CONFIG A — LOI ONLY (first contact, rolling programs)
1. **Cover email** — 3-4 sentences max. Who you are, what you're requesting, why you're writing. Professional, not pleading.
2. **LOI as PDF attachment** — 2-3 pages. Atlas produces this. Dark template NOT used here — standard white professional document for external submissions.
3. **IRS Determination Letter** — attached as PDF (proves 501c3 status)

#### CONFIG B — LOI + SUPPORTING DOCS (mid-stage, invited applications)
1. **Cover email** — brief, professional
2. **LOI as PDF** — 2-3 pages (Atlas)
3. **One-page organizational overview** — E5 at a glance (Atlas)
4. **IRS Determination Letter**
5. **Board list** — per funder requirements
6. **Most recent financials** — year-end or audited statements

#### CONFIG C — FULL PROPOSAL (invited, post-LOI acceptance)
1. **Cover letter** (1 page — customized per funder)
2. **Executive Summary** (1 page)
3. **Need Statement** (2-3 pages with data, citations)
4. **Project Description** — methods, timeline, activities (3-4 pages)
5. **Evaluation Plan** — how we measure success (1-2 pages)
6. **Organizational Capacity** — who we are, track record (1-2 pages)
7. **Project Budget** — detailed, line-item (1-2 pages)
8. **Budget Narrative** — explains every line (1 page)
9. **Attachments** — IRS letter, audited financials, board list, key staff bios, letters of support

### The LOI Document Standard (Atlas Build Spec)
- **Format:** 8.5x11, 1-inch margins, 11pt serif body font (Georgia or Times), 12pt headers
- **Length:** Strictly 2-3 pages. Never exceed.
- **Header:** Organization name, address, date, funder name and address (formal business letter format)
- **Sections:**
  1. Opening hook (2-3 sentences) — the problem, stated with precision and urgency
  2. Our solution (1 paragraph) — what we do, how it works, why it's different
  3. The ask (1 paragraph) — specific dollar amount, specific use, specific timeframe
  4. Organizational credentials (1 paragraph) — 501c3, key credentials, track record
  5. Closing (2-3 sentences) — invite conversation, next step
  6. Signature block — Israel Armstead, Executive Director, E5 Enclave Inc.
- **Tone:** Sovereign. Confident. Never begging. We are offering partnership, not requesting charity.
- **Voice check (LOGOS):** Black-first framing. Precision over hyperbole. No over-claiming.
- **File naming:** `E5_Enclave_LOI_[FunderName]_[YYYYMMDD].pdf`

## PART 4 — VOICE STANDARD (LOGOS Doctrine)

### What "Sovereign Voice" Means in a Grant Context
Program officers read hundreds of LOIs. Most sound the same: desperate, self-deprecating, overwrought. The sovereign voice is different — it reads like a peer conversation between two organizations that share a mission.

**DO:**
- State the problem with data and precision. Not emotion.
- State our solution with confidence. We know this works.
- Frame the funder's investment as a partnership, not a lifeline.
- Use their exact priority language, mirrored back. Show you read them.
- Close with a clear, calm next step.

**DO NOT:**
- Use words like "humbly," "honored," "grateful for the opportunity"
- Over-explain the organization. Two sentences on who we are, not two paragraphs.
- Use generic statistics that any org could use. Use our specific data.
- Make claims we cannot substantiate. PROOF gates every factual statement.
- Use "unique" or "first-of-its-kind" without proof. We can say "world's first agentic-first staffed NGO" because it's true and we declared it.

### The LOGOS 5-Point Soul Check
Before any LOI is submitted, LOGOS confirms:
1. Black-first framing — the Black community is centered, not described as a problem
2. No subliminal deficit framing — we describe assets and power, not pathology
3. Sovereign voice — we are offering partnership, not begging
4. Precision — every claim is specific and verifiable
5. "By grace, perfect ways" closes every communication

## PART 5 — FUNDER PIPELINE ARCHITECTURE

### Tier 1 — OPEN & ELIGIBLE (active pursuit)
Funders with open programs, clear eligibility match, email or portal submission.
Percy drafts. Miranda reviews. LOGOS clears. Percy submits.

### Tier 2 — RELATIONSHIP-FIRST (cultivation pipeline)
Invitation-only funders, warm-intro-required funders, or funders where we need a prior grantee connection.
Scout builds the relationship map. Hermes manages introductions. DEAL tracks conversations.
**Examples:** Jessie Ball duPont (invitation only), Ford Foundation (relationship preferred)
**Never send cold to these.** Build the relationship first.

### Tier 3 — ELIGIBILITY MISMATCH (archived)
Funders whose requirements we do not meet. Do not submit. Do not draft. Archive with explanation.
**Examples:** Koch Foundation (Catholic only), any funder requiring specific denominational affiliation

### Tier 4 — CLOSED PROGRAMS (archived with watch)
Programs that are temporarily or permanently closed. Archive with re-open date if available.
**Examples:** MacArthur Criminal Justice Safety and Justice Challenge (closed permanently)
Scout sets a watch — if new program opens, surfaces it immediately.
