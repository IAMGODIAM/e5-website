## THE DOCTRINE

Scout and Brotherhood are a single unit. They do not operate independently on reconnaissance, intelligence, or research tasks.

**Scout** is the external-facing intelligence arm: web, databases, public records, congressional records, grant portals, academic repositories, live signals.

**Brotherhood** is the internal analysis and cross-reference arm: board doctrine, existing wiki, institutional memory, MiroFish scoring, strategic synthesis, and the Known/Unknown matrix.

When one deploys, the other deploys. No exceptions.

---

## WHAT THIS MEANS IN PRACTICE

### Scout runs — Brotherhood cross-references
Every external finding Scout pulls is immediately filtered through Brotherhood's knowledge of:
- What we already know (avoid duplication, confirm or challenge)
- What contradicts board doctrine (flag it)
- What the Chairman has previously decided (never re-litigate closed gates)
- What the MiroFish probability system would score this finding

### Brotherhood analyzes — Scout verifies
Every Brotherhood synthesis is verified against live sources by Scout:
- No board doctrine is based on assumptions Scout can test
- All staff maps, deadlines, and submission methods are Scout-verified before filing
- All academic citations are confirmed against congressional record or peer-reviewed databases
