<p class="first">Block to the Boardroom is the Enclave's flagship Engagement program — a structured cohort pipeline that moves community members from *organized neighborhoods* into *organized institutional capacity*. Every rung of the pipeline is named, mentored, and accountable. The premise is unremarkable but rare in practice: *the people closest to the work belong in the rooms where the decisions are made.*</p>

## The pipeline

The program is structured as a four-rung cohort pipeline:

- **Rung I — Neighborhood organizing.** Foundational organizer training; door-knocking, listening circles, issue identification, base-building.
- **Rung II — Civic-literacy intensive.** Municipal and state government mechanics, the legislative process, budget cycles, the federal register, executive rule-making.
- **Rung III — Policy authorship.** Translating community priorities into draft policy. Co-authoring with allied legislators, agency staff, and policy researchers. Publishing in *The Record* where the work is institutional.
- **Rung IV — Board service and elected office.** Mentored placement on nonprofit boards, advisory bodies, and (where the cohort member chooses) electoral candidacies.

Mentors at every rung. Cohort solidarity across all four. Alumni pipelines back into the next cohort as mentors and as anchors.

## Pillar alignment

Engagement pillar. Adjacencies: Economic (cohort members move into governance roles in cooperative enterprises), Educational (the curriculum is taught at McCartney Academy and via Enclave open courses), Emancipated (R246 and other campaigns recruit policy-author cohort members).

## Roadmap

- **Phase I (2026):** founding cohort recruited (Liberty City + adjacent neighborhoods); Rung I and Rung II curriculum delivered; mentor network seated.
- **Phase II (2027):** first cohort completes Rungs III and IV; second cohort begins; alumni pipeline established.
- **Phase III (2028+):** replicable cohort kit published; partner cities adopt under a permissive franchise.

## Doctrine

*Civic literacy without policy authorship is theatre. Policy authorship without neighborhood mandate is technocracy.* Block to the Boardroom refuses both, and builds the pipeline that makes neither possible to confuse with the other.
