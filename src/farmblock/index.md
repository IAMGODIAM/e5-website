<p class="first">FarmBlock is E5 Enclave's flagship Environmental program — a cooperative urban farming platform combining food production, workforce training, and agricultural technology in Liberty City, Miami. FarmBlock turns vacant land into sovereign food infrastructure. It turns food deserts into food systems. It turns community members into farmers, technologists, and cooperative owners.</p>

---

## The Problem

Liberty City is a food desert. The USDA defines a food desert as a low-income area where a substantial number of residents have low access to a supermarket or large grocery store. By that definition, the community that produced E5 Enclave has been structurally denied basic food access for decades.

This is not a natural condition. It is a policy condition — produced by redlining, disinvestment, and the systematic removal of grocery infrastructure from Black neighborhoods that began after urban renewal in the 1950s and continued through the present.

FarmBlock is the structural response.

---

## What FarmBlock Builds

**Cooperative Urban Farms.** Distributed plots across Liberty City — managed as worker cooperatives, owned by the community members who work them, governed by the Coalition's cooperative ownership doctrine.

**Workforce Development.** A structured training pipeline from basic agricultural literacy through advanced ag-tech, urban hydroponic systems, vertical farming, and cooperative business management. Every rung mentored. Every graduate a potential cooperative owner.

**Agricultural Technology.** FarmBlock is a technology platform, not just a farm program. Sensor networks, soil data, yield analytics, and the FarmBlock FDI (Food Desert Index) — a sovereign dataset measuring food access by census tract for Black communities across the United States.

**The FarmBlock FDI Dataset.** 50 priority cities. Food access, demographic, and infrastructure data. The FDI is the Environmental pillar's answer to the BDI Dataset — measuring the food dimension of structural distress with the same rigor applied to wealth, health, and criminal justice.

---

## The FarmBlock Approach

FarmBlock operates on three principles:

**Sovereignty over dependency.** Community-owned cooperative farms produce food that is distributed within the community first, sold outside it second. No extractive ownership. No outside landlord. The land and its yield belong to the people who work it.

**Technology as equalizer.** The same precision agriculture tools used by industrial farms are available to cooperative urban farms when the data infrastructure is built correctly. FarmBlock builds that infrastructure — making yield data, soil analytics, and market intelligence available to cooperative farmers in Liberty City.

**Replication as doctrine.** Every model FarmBlock develops in Liberty City is documented, open-sourced, and made freely available for other communities to adapt. The goal is not one farm. The goal is a network of sovereign food infrastructure across every city where the BDI signals food apartheid.

## Current Status

- **Phase I (2026):** Site identification, community organizing, cooperative structure established, first training cohort recruited in Liberty City.

- **Phase III (2028+):** Replication kit published; partner cities adopt cooperative farming model under permissive franchise.

## The Data Anchor

FarmBlock is one of the eight pillars of the BDI Sovereign Dataset. The Environmental pillar tracks:

- Food desert exposure by Black census tract
- Black farmer count and land loss (USDA NASS, 1910–2022)
- Toxic facility proximity and air quality by Black community
- Environmental burden scores (EPA EJScreen)
- Grocery store access per capita in Black ZIP codes

That data — collected, verified, and published openly by E5 Enclave — is the evidentiary foundation for everything FarmBlock builds.
