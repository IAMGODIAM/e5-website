## Abstract

*Parallel Negros* is the first dispatch from the E5 think tank — a foundational comparative work that examines the parallel architectures of citizenship, capital, and coercion that have shaped the lineage's path through American history.

The dispatch argues that what looks, in policy debate, like a single American story is in fact two parallel architectures running in tension: one extending the franchise, the other restricting it; one accumulating capital, the other extracting it; one building institutions, the other dismantling them. The lineage has lived inside both architectures simultaneously — the formal one, on paper; the parallel one, in practice.

The institutional remedy that follows from this analysis is the work the Enclave is built to convene: institutions *built by and for the lineage*, calibrated to the parallel reality, not the paper one.
