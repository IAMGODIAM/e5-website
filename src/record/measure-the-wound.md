## Abstract

*Measure the Wound* is the foundational record of harms — a systematic, dual-source-verified documentation of the named injuries the lineage has sustained from the colonial period to the present. It is the evidentiary foundation against which every E5 program and every E5 campaign is calibrated.

The record is organized by category — wage loss, opportunity loss, land loss, life and limb, family separation, cultural theft, and the open category of *unrepaired others* — with a methodology section per category and a published changelog of revisions. Where the evidence permits, claims carry dual-source verification. Where the evidence is contested or incomplete, the record names the contestation rather than smoothing past it.

The doctrine the record encodes is simple: *what is measured cannot be dismissed*. The work of building the alternative — the institutions, the programs, the campaigns — proceeds only on a foundation of evidence we will defend in any room.

## Editorial position in The Record

Measure the Wound is № II in The Record · Volume I. It is the document the Enclave returns to most often — the source of every campaign's evidentiary anchor and the standard against which new campaigns are evaluated for inclusion.
