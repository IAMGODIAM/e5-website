## Abstract

*Restitution 246* is the campaign framework for one disciplined lane within the larger reparations question: a wage-loss and opportunity-loss accounting for the 246 years of stolen labor under American chattel slavery — and for the inherent loss of opportunity that theft caused.

The framework is structured around *the Five Questions* (the doctrinal anchor), the Lloyd Kelly accounting framework, and the Craemer (2015) wage-hour methodology. The mechanism it proposes is a $120T bond held in perpetuity, with interest payments directed to the lineage as partial restitution.

R246 names what it is not. It does not claim to settle the full reparations question. It does not address pain and suffering, racial terror, family separation, land theft, cultural theft, or every harm that followed slavery. *Those injuries require their own frameworks.* R246 is one lane, named with discipline.

Restitution 246 is № III in The Record · Volume I. It is also the lead campaign under the [Emancipated pillar](/pillars/emancipated/), and a central interest of the Enclave's policy and partnership work.
