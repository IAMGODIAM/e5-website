§01

The Big Short lens

In 2007 the index made new highs while the foundation rotted. A handful of people read the foundation instead of the index — and sized their conviction to the gap between price and reality.

Michael Burry didn't short the housing market because it had already fallen. He shorted it because the price hadn't caught up to the truth yet — the deterioration was visible in the data months before it was visible in the tape. That is the exact discipline here. The headline index is the 2007 home-price chart: serene. The names underneath are the subprime tranches: already cracking.

The operating principle

"Don't short the crash. Short the denial. The money is made in the distance between what the price says and what the cash flows already know."
